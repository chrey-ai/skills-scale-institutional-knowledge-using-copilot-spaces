# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This README serves as the central index for OctoAcme's project management process documentation. It provides a high-level overview of how OctoAcme runs projects—from initial idea through retrospective—and links to each detailed process document for easy reference.

## Overview

OctoAcme uses a lightweight, iterative project management approach grounded in customer value, clear ownership, and measurable success. Projects move through five key lifecycle phases: **Initiation** (validating the business need, aligning stakeholders, and defining success criteria), **Planning** (building the backlog, estimating effort, aligning milestones, and identifying risks), **Execution & Tracking** (iterative delivery with twice-weekly standups, regular demos, QA validation, and continuous risk monitoring), **Release & Deployment** (standardized release process with staged rollouts and incident-response runbooks), and **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement** (capturing learnings and driving concrete action items).

Key roles span the full delivery team. **Project Managers** coordinate schedules, risks, and cross-team communications. **Product Managers** own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes. **Developers** implement features and maintain quality through code review and testing. **QA/Testing** validates acceptance criteria and quality standards. **Stakeholders** provide approvals, inputs, and prioritization guidance at key decision gates.

OctoAcme maintains a structured communication cadence: twice-weekly standups for the delivery team, weekly syncs between the PM and Product Manager, and monthly stakeholder updates. Risks are tracked in a living Risk Register and escalate from the team level → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor. Security incidents follow a dedicated runbook with immediate on-call notification.

Quality assurance is integrated throughout the lifecycle, not bolted on at the end. Developers write and maintain tests alongside their code, acceptance criteria are defined during planning, and QA validates each increment before release. Post-release, blameless retrospectives and measurable action items drive continuous process improvement across the team.
